Largest planet in the Solar System, both in mass and diameter, Jupiter is the fifth closest to the sun. Jupiter is a gaseous planet, just like Neptune, Saturn and Uranus. The planet Jupiter has a mass 318 times greater than that of planet earth. Callisto, Europa and Ganymede are Jupiter's best-known satellites, with 67 confirmed satellites.

After a five-year journey, in 2016 the Juno spacecraft entered the orbit of the planet Jupiter. The great red spot that is the most striking feature of Jupiter, is a countercyclical storm that is located 22 ° south of the equator with about 40 thousand km in length. The gravity of Jupiter is 24.79 m / s².

Its atmosphere is composed of:

Hydrogen - 75%
Helium - 24%

Ammonia and methane are also found in its atmosphere.
